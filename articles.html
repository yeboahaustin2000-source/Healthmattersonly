Acute Tonsillitis

Acute tonsillitis is inflammation of the tonsils caused by viral or bacterial infection. It is common in children and young adults.

Causes

  • Viral infections
  • Bacterial infections (Streptococcus)

Symptoms

  • Sore throat
  • Fever
  • Difficulty swallowing
  • Swollen lymph nodes

Treatment

  • Rest
  • Warm fluids
  • Pain medication
  • Antibiotics (if bacterial)

Test Your Understanding

Q: What part of the body is affected in tonsillitis?
A: The tonsils.

Q: Do all tonsillitis cases need antibiotics?
A: No, only bacterial infections need antibiotics.

Drowning Emergency First Aid

Drowning occurs when water prevents breathing, leading to lack of oxygen to the brain.

Emergency Steps

  1. Remove person from water safely
  2. Check breathing
  3. Call emergency services
  4. Start CPR if not breathing
  5. Keep patient warm

Test Your Understanding

Q: What is the first thing to check after removing from water?
A: Breathing.

Q: Why is oxygen important?
A: The brain needs oxygen to survive.

Sickle Cell Crisis Home Care

Sickle cell crisis is a painful episode caused by blocked blood flow.

Home Management

  • Drink plenty of fluids
  • Keep warm
  • Take prescribed pain medication
  • Avoid stress and cold

When To Go To Hospital

  • Severe pain
  • Difficulty breathing
  • Fever
  • Extreme weakness

Test Your Understanding

Q: Name one trigger of sickle cell crisis.
A: Cold exposure, dehydration or stress.

Q: Why is hydration important?
A: It helps blood flow properly.
